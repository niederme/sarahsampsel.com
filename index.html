The Washington Post

Director of Digital Strategy & Design 2013 – 2015

I opened a product design office in NYC to lead redesign efforts for the Washington Post's web platform. We worked on scope, strategy and execution of a site-wide rethink involving CMS capabilities as well as consumer-facing experiences.

I built the team from scratch, hiring design and strategy veterans from different industries with experience in solving complex design challenges, frontend development, research, UX, and product development.

We collaborated closely with the newsroom, engineering and advertising on developing a product roadmap and implemented design sprints to generate ideas and concepts for user testing.

We designed plans and interviews for user testing sessions and developed recruiting strategies for both internal and external testers.

I also worked on design strategy and assisted in hiring for the Coral Project, a collaboration between the Washington Post, the New York-Times, the Knight Foundation and Mozilla to build an open-source commenting platform.

Director of Digital Design 2011 – 2013

I was a member of the core leadership team in the newsroom that focused on setting strategy and priorities for company-wide initiatives.

I built and managed a nimble 10-person team focused on digital, mobile and new product design, and created positions for and hired specialists in user experience, interaction design and development, motion graphics and visual design.

My team launched a site-wide redesign in 2011, including a new CMS, and a design refresh in 2014.

As readership habits changed and technology evolved, I had the honor of being a key voice in how to rethink core news products. I conceptualized and designed a live events platform, the politics app for Election 2012, an enterprise story template and created and evolved the designs for the iPad app.

Worked in partnership with companies like Google, Tumblr, Storify, Twitter and Facebook on creating new products for news consumption.

Had the privilege of working on digital storytelling methods and presentations for award-winning journalism projects that millions of people see. Some examples include Top Secret America, Muslims In America and two presidential elections.

Ethos

As someone who has worked brand-side and now agency-side, I think about digital products in a holistic way – how to create the best possible solution, how it performs and how it will impact revenue. My experience at Work & Co, and previously at the Washington Post, has given me a unique outlook – developing new concepts versus building upon existing ones is vastly different, but I find that thinking from both ends of that spectrum can enhance the process.

A Short Bio

I was born in Erie, PA, one of the snowiest cities in the world. I was raised to think creatively, love the outdoors, take adventures, and eat Polish food. I’m still that, for the most part, but now I’m based in Brooklyn, NY with John and Katya. Prior to New York, I lived in Washington D.C. for several years and met many amazing lifelong friends.
